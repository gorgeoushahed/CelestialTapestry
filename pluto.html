Pluto


Pluto is a small, icy world that's located in the outer reaches of the solar system. It was once considered the ninth planet in our solar system, but it was reclassified as a "dwarf planet" by the International Astronomical Union in 2006. Despite its small size, Pluto is a fascinating place, with a surface that's covered in mountains, valleys, and plains. It has five known moons, each with its own unique features and characteristics. Despite its distance from the sun, Pluto is an active and dynamic world, with surface features that suggest a complex and varied geology. It's a place that's still largely unknown to us, and as we continue to study this distant world, we're sure to uncover even more amazing secrets.
